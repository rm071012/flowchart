Select type of Surgery

Minor Surgery

Continue warfarin and adjust dose to achieve INR target of 2.0

(Unless regional anaesthesia contemplated where a target of 1.5 may be appropriate

Major Surgery

Stop warfarin 5 days before operation and then consider risk/benefit profile(as below)

Surgery can be commenced if INR<= 1.5 on the day of surgery.

Major Surgery

Select the indication for Warfarin therapy

VTE

If....

VTE within previous 3 months

Very high risk patients with a previous VTE whilst on therapeutic anticoagulation who now have a target INR of 3.5

Then High Risk for VTE

Bridging therapy 1 preoperatively and Regime 1 post-operatively

Atrial Fibrillation

If.....

Patients with a previous stroke or TIA in last 3 months

Patients with a previous stroke or TIA and three or more of the following risk factor :

CCF, HYPERTENSION, AGE > 75 , DIABETES MELLITUS

Then High Risk for VTE

Bridging therapy 1 preoperatively and Regime 1 post-operatively

Mechanical Heart Valves

If...

MHV patients other than those with a bileaflet aortic valve and no other risk factors

Then High Risk for VTE

Bridging therapy 2 preoperatively and Regime 2 post-operatively

Bridging therapy 1

Pre operative Bridging Therapy 1

DAY Warfarin LMWH
Day6 Take usual dose of warfarin
Day 5 Stop Warfarin
Day 4 Stop Warfarin
Day 3 Stop Warfarin Give LMWH (1.5mg/kg OD)
Day 2 Stop Warfarin Give LMWH (1.5mg/kg OD)
Day 1 Stop Warfarin Give LMWH (40mg OD)
Day of surgery Check INR Follow Regime 1

Post operative Regime 1

Wait 6 hours post-op

Check with surgeon for risk of bleeding

Commence therapeutic dose of Enoxaparin 1.5mg/kg s/c OD

But If patient had epidural OR high risk of surgical bleeding...

Then prophylactic dose of Enoxaparin 40mg

restart warfarin once patient can take orally
